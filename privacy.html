Privacy Policy

Effective date: 1 April 2026

WalkPhase is a pedestrian crossing safety app for iPhone and Apple Watch. We built it to help you cross the road safely — not to collect your data. This policy explains exactly what happens with your information when you use the app.

  • Your crossing data stays on your device. We never upload your GPS tracks, accelerometer readings, or intersection list.
  • No accounts, no sign-in, no passwords. WalkPhase does not know who you are.
  • No advertising. No tracking. No data sales. We do not use ad SDKs, the IDFA, or fingerprinting of any kind.
  • Anonymous analytics only. We count events (like "a crossing was completed") — never locations or identities.

1. Data that never leaves your device

The following data is created and stored entirely on your iPhone or Apple Watch. WalkPhase does not transmit, upload, or have any server-side access to it:

DataPurpose
GPS location tracksRecord your route during a crossing
Accelerometer & motion dataGait analysis and walking/stationary detection
Crossing recordsTrack your crossing history and calculate safety verdicts
Intersection listStore your saved crossings
Signal timing plansLearn and predict pedestrian signal phases
Debug logsOn-device troubleshooting only
Mobility profile (if set)Personalise clearance calculations

This data is yours. It lives on your device and in your personal iCloud account. We cannot see it, request it, or retrieve it.

2. Anonymous analytics

We use TelemetryDeck, an EU-based, privacy-first analytics service, to understand how WalkPhase is used in aggregate. TelemetryDeck processes data in Germany and is fully GDPR-compliant.

What we receive:

  • App opened, onboarding completed
  • Crossing completed (duration and safety verdict — no coordinates)
  • Metro area (city name only, determined once on first launch)

How identification is prevented:

  • TelemetryDeck uses double-hashed anonymous identifiers. They cannot identify individual users, and neither can we.
  • No Apple Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA) is collected.
  • No device fingerprinting is performed.

3. Safety reports

WalkPhase lets you email safety reports to road authorities. When you choose to send a report:

  • The email is composed and sent through your own Mail app on your device.
  • WalkPhase does not see, store, or process the email content.
  • WalkPhase is CC'd on the report (team@walkphase.com) so we can track which crossings users are raising concerns about. You can remove the CC before sending.

No report is ever sent without your explicit action.

4. Permissions

PermissionWhy we need it
Location (Always)Geofence detection allows automatic crossing tracking even in the background. You can revoke this at any time.
Motion & FitnessDetects whether you are walking or stationary, and records accelerometer data for gait analysis.
NotificationsApproach alerts, crossing results, and optional weekly summaries.

You can deny or revoke any permission at any time. WalkPhase will still function with reduced capability.

5. iCloud backup

Intersection data and crossing records are backed up to your personal iCloud account. Accelerometer data is not backed up (it exceeds iCloud size limits). This is your personal iCloud storage — WalkPhase has no access to your iCloud account.

6. What we do NOT do

  • We do not create user accounts or profiles.
  • We do not collect your name, email, or any personal identifiers.
  • We do not use advertising SDKs or display ads.
  • We do not use the IDFA or any cross-app tracking.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties.
  • We do not use fingerprinting techniques.
  • We have no social features that could expose your identity.

7. Future: community data

In a future update, WalkPhase may offer an optional feature to share anonymous, aggregated signal timing data. If introduced:

  • Only aggregated timing data would be shared (e.g., "average walk phase is 12 seconds").
  • No individual crossing records, GPS tracks, or personal information would be included.
  • The feature would be opt-in with a clear toggle.

This policy will be updated before any such feature is released.

8. Children's privacy

WalkPhase does not knowingly collect any personal information from children under 16. Since the app collects no personal information from anyone, no age-gating is required.

9. Your rights under GDPR

WalkPhase is developed in Dublin, Ireland. Because almost all data remains on your device, your rights are largely self-service:

  • Access & portability: Your crossing data is on your device. You own it.
  • Deletion: Delete the app to remove all local data.
  • Analytics opt-out: Contact us and we will exclude your session from future processing.

If you have any GDPR-related request, contact us and we will respond within 30 days.

10. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update the effective date and notify users via an in-app notice. The current version is always available at walkphase.com/privacy.
